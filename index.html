Research Interests

My research focuses on the behavior and performance of cementitious materials, with particular attention to low-carbon and blended systems. I combine experimental techniques such as thermogravimetric analysis, micro-indentation, and mechanical testing to characterize hydration, strength, and durability. In parallel, I develop micromechanical models to predict the mechanical properties and evolution of microstructure over time. This multi-scale approach allows for a better understanding of how material composition and curing conditions influence macroscopic properties (chloride diffusion, permeability or strength development,...). The goal is to support the development of sustainable, high-performance concretes through a deeper scientific understanding of their behavior.
