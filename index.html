Research Interests

My research focuses on the behavior and performance of cementitious materials, with particular attention to low-carbon and blended systems. I combine experimental techniques such as thermogravimetric analysis, optical and scanning electron microscopy observations, nano and micro-indentation, and mechanical testing to characterize hydration, strength, and durability of cementitious materials. In parallel, I develop theoretical models based on micromechanical concepts to predict the mechanical properties and the evolution of the microstructure over time. This multi-scale approach allows for a better understanding of how material composition and curing conditions influence macroscopic properties. The goal is to support the development of sustainable, high-performance concretes through a deeper scientific understanding of their behavior.
